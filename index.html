CHAPTER 1

On the final day of my sophomore year of high school, I was hit in the face with a baseball bat. Something in my classmate’s swing slipped, and the bat came crashing into my face at full speed.

I have no memory of the moment of impact. I took the bat directly between the eyes. The collision crushed my nose into a deformed “U” shape.

When the bat made contact with my face, the soft tissue of my brain slammed against the inside of my skull. Immediately, I was overwhelmed by swelling.

Within seconds, I had a broken nose, multiple skull fractures, and two shattered eye sockets. My morning had started so normally.
